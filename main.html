Bengalore


Tourist Attractions in Bengalore. Bengalore city has a number of tourist places. Apart from the numerous gardens, it also has some historical sites and other places of interest. The Vidhan Soudha or the State Secretariat, built in 1954, is the main attraction of this city.

  1. Lalbagh Botanical Garden: A 240-acre garden that is home to over 1,000 species of plants, trees, and flowers. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Bangalore and is known for its beautiful glasshouse.
  2. Bangalore Palace: A stunning example of Tudor-style architecture, the Bangalore Palace is a must-visit destination for history buffs. It was built in the 19th century and is known for its beautiful carvings, paintings, and artwork.
  3. Cubbon Park: A sprawling park located in the heart of the city, Cubbon Park is a great place to relax and unwind. It is home to numerous species of trees and plants and has a number of walking paths, statues, and fountains.
  4. Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace: This beautiful palace was built by Tipu Sultan, a ruler of the Kingdom of Mysore, in 1791. It is known for its beautiful architecture and is a popular tourist destination in Bangalore.
  5. ISKCON Temple: The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) has a beautiful temple in Bangalore that is known for its intricate carvings and peaceful atmosphere. It is a must-visit destination for spiritual seekers.
  • Masala Dosa: A crispy and savory pancake made from rice and lentil batter, filled with a spiced potato mixture.
  • Vada: A deep-fried doughnut-shaped snack made from lentil batter, served with coconut chutney and sambar.
  • Idli: A steamed rice and lentil cake, served with coconut chutney and sambar.
  • Puliyogare: A spicy and tangy rice dish made with tamarind, peanuts, and a special spice blend.
  • Kesari Bath: A sweet and fragrant dessert made with semolina, sugar, ghee, and saffron.
